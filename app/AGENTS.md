# Prototype Instructions

- Station-worker NPC standard: the roguelike world is an abandoned night train network combining urban legend and folk-horror. Non-hostile workers are familiar with local routes, trade and rules; they must read as unsettling but approachable contacts rather than hostile monsters. Keep all worker art in high-detail 16/32-bit dark pixel treatment (black/old brown/bone/olive palette, top-left or front-top light, material-separated clothing/leather/rust/glass/paper/wet skin) and avoid gore, chibi/flat/modern-bright/photoreal styling, pure silhouettes, background-baked character props, watermarks or cropped feet. Every worker owns a distinct functional prop, clear foot baseline, map-side scene cue, interaction pocket and future hooks for portrait, lit variant, four-way walk and a simple work animation.
- NPC source-art and placement update: the sixteen supplied final transparent `npc-*.png` images are copied into `src/assets/npcs`, bound one-to-one to their matching catalog archetypes and inlined for the file:// package. The world renderer preserves their original face, uniform and held item, anchoring all feet to the shared map baseline; only a separate map-layer scene cue is added behind them. Each live map uses an authored cue-keyed anchor table, placing a worker beside an appropriate existing object (gate, signal box, tool cart, pump, luggage/locker, mirror portal, control panel or train door) and away from the central kite loop, cache/drop marker, exits and spawn zones. The boarding scene now renders the selected protagonist sprite as the passenger and the actual matching NPC sprite as the receiver.
- Worksite NPC prototype: `stationNpcs.js` now centralizes sixteen named non-hostile worker archetypes across shop, route, function and story categories. Every campaign stage spawns an in-world contact plus a map-specific host at authored side-pocket anchors rather than enemy spawns, fire lanes or chokepoints. The canvas draws a material-layered stationary worker, its separate scene cue and a nearby `E` prompt; interaction opens a paused dialogue card and is available via a mobile control. Existing train interludes use the same fully colored worker anatomy instead of a pure black silhouette.
- Wave-skip playtest update: the combat header exposes a clearly marked `跳过波次` test control. One press resolves only the active wave, clears remaining combat objects, and uses the normal reward/relic/train/end flow. It disables outside active combat, so it cannot bypass a selection, transfer or result panel.

Run the local server yourself and open the preview in the browser available to this environment. Do not give the user server-start instructions when you can run it.

Before making substantial visual changes, use the Product Design plugin's `get-context` skill when the visual source is unclear or no longer matches the current goal. When the user gives durable prototype-specific design feedback, preferences, or decisions, record them in `AGENTS.md`.

When implementing from a selected generated mock, treat that image as the source of truth for layout, component anatomy, density, spacing, color, typography, visible content, and hierarchy.

## Active prototype decisions

- Selected visual direction: the rain-dark subway observation screen featuring the human-scale "血线维修员".
- The other world is inhabited by ordinary humanoid workers in the middle of mundane jobs; avoid oversized gods or battle scenes.
- Horror belongs to fictional workers and their environment. Uploaded real-person photos are only observation anchors and must never be demeaned or bloodied.
- The tone is mature, uncanny industrial horror with implied blood traces and biological anomalies, not graphic gore.
- Build and verify the core mobile journey at 390 × 844: consent → anchor → choices → observation record → private archive.
- The scenario route is a four-question “彼界心境问卷”: scenario responses route users to fictional residents and images, never to personality, mental-health, or appearance judgments. The fourth signal chooses the main or parallel resident branch.
- Current routes: 修复、引路、归档、巡界、夜市及其五条彼侧分支，共 10 名居民。The narrative dossier remains visible; “保存无文字原图” exports only the selected resident image.
- The route images use an original paper-theater Japanese urban-horror direction: hand-cut paper layers, rough ink contours, faded indigo/tobacco/cream/vermilion, paper fibers and theatrical shadows. Do not directly copy existing horror-animation characters or scenes.
- Residents should have clearly visible, highly exaggerated fictional features (oversized eyes, stretched smiles, skewed proportions) while remaining non-graphic; reference photos are mood/anatomy references only, never cloned.
- Visual direction is intentionally multi-world rather than uniform: dossier ink, illustrative/cartoon uncanny, industrial grotesque, folk-surreal, and calm cinematic realism can coexist. Realistic portraits may use very grotesque exaggerated facial anatomy, provided they remain fictional and avoid graphic injury or gore.
- The MBTI catalog is a separate opt-in 16-question scenario questionnaire. It maps voluntarily submitted answers through four weighted dimensions (+2/+1/-1/-2) to an MBTI-style fictional projection, but must never infer personality from photos, appearance, uploaded anchors, or behavior. It contains 16 fictional parallel residents and clearly states that it is entertainment, not psychological assessment.
- Each MBTI resident includes three narrative personality traits, an in-world bodily or environmental manifestation, and a night-shift habit. These describe the fictional resident only; frame them as a "性格投影" and keep the entertainment/non-diagnostic boundary visible.
- The MBTI record offers a separate deep-report view for every one of the 16 types. Its template includes the four answer-axis scores, MBTI-community cognitive-function narration, common patterns, pressure responses, practical reminders, collaboration/work-environment notes, and the fictional resident's other-world mapping. It must state that this is optional self-exploration, not a scientific brain model, diagnosis, or fixed definition of the player.
- Role-play expansion: every one of the 16 MBTI-style fictional residents has a replayable, data-driven four-stage “彼界夜班” first-night chapter. Every stage presents three story-grounded choices with a concise narrative consequence, and prior choices remain visible as the night-log path. A disclosed in-world rule plus the accumulated safe choices determine one of two fictional outcomes: “存活” or “死亡”; the final-stage forbidden option is explicitly fatal. Every ending must include a generated, path-specific conclusion plus a four-step debrief stating the selected action, its in-world effect, and its contribution to the return-or-loss outcome. The death state is a non-graphic, fictional character outcome only; it never represents, predicts, or refers to the real user. ISFJ's 雾灯引路员 uses the custom “第一夜：末班之后” platform scene; the other chapters intentionally reuse their dossier art until their dedicated scene art is added. Keep the player role distinct from the real user and contain the horror in the fictional world.
- Exploration loop: completing a night can be saved into the local-only “彼界世界线档案馆”. Every resident has two fictional artifact variants (returned / lost), one named relationship line to another resident, and a second-night teaser. Lost routes expose a “残响追查” screen; both returned and lost artifacts can open the linked resident’s night shift without asserting a new MBTI result. Persist only fictional world-line data, choices, and artifacts—never uploaded photos, face cues, or questionnaire answers.
- Survival-expedition extension: the MBTI-style fictional resident can enter a replayable first chapter, “雾港末班车：第七站无人下车”. It uses four node-based choices, stamina / clarity / mutation state, traceable artifacts, and projection-only shift gifts tied to voluntary questionnaire letters. Outcomes are fictional “归还” or “失联” world-line results; never frame these mechanics as real personality ability, diagnosis, or prediction.
- World-map and residue-mail extension: locally archive each completed expedition into the Fog Harbor map and a shareable fictional route code. The code may contain only outcome and abstract A/B/C route symbols; it must never encode or expose photos, names, MBTI-style type, questionnaire answers, device data, or real-world identity. Importing a code only unlocks an in-world letter and map marker.
- Roguelike extension: after completing the optional questionnaire, the generated fictional resident can enter the replayable “雾港清障·肉鸽试行”, a real-time 2D arena MVP. It contains WASD / touch movement, pointer-directed automatic fire, Space / touch dash, three initial weapons, five ordinary anomaly types, an elite chance, one Boss, 20+ data-driven three-choice upgrades, a random event, a shop, and local-only “余烬核心” meta progression capped at +15 initial state. The resident is a parallel-world projection, never the real user; confrontation and failure are non-graphic fictional world-line outcomes, and questionnaire letters only shape fictional starting techniques rather than real skills, personality strength, or combat aptitude.
- Arena presentation update: the playable unit is a clearly readable, type-specific miniature model of the selected fictional dossier resident (large rimmed eyes, hair/antenna variant, long uniform with seams, work belt, badge, type token, and varied silhouette), never an uploaded real-person image. Hostile units use a separate family of anomalous station-worker NPC models. The arena is a 1680×1080 world rendered through a 560×360 follow-camera with minimap; three layouts place 16 industrial obstacles across the world. Train husks, pillars, freight crates, cable channels, and barricades block player movement, hostile movement, and projectiles so pathing and line-of-fire choices matter.
- Weapon-avatar asset update: the three user-supplied, fictional pixel-character sprites are the roguelike's primary playable protagonist model, not overlays on the former generic resident. Each opening weapon selects the protagonist's current pose: pulse pistol → raised pistol pose, mag scatter → two-handed scattergun pose, drone emitter → glowing emitter/orb pose. Their green preview backgrounds are chroma-keyed at runtime from inline local assets, leaving the original image files unchanged and ensuring the standalone file:// package does not silently fall back to the former generic model. The resident's MBTI-style type token remains a fictional in-world marker below the sprite; the asset does not depict or derive from the real user.
- Roguelike quick-entry and scale update: the observation landing page exposes a direct "enter roguelike" button that launches a default fictional Fog Harbor resident with the pulse-pistol pose and does not infer or claim a questionnaire/personality result. The three supplied protagonist sprites use a small battlefield scale and collision radius comparable to ordinary station-worker NPCs, rather than a giant hero presentation.
- Roguelike aim-rig update: supplied protagonist sprites retain their original silhouette, but their combat presentation adds a lightweight canvas arm-and-weapon rig. The body leans and faces the pointer/touch aim, the weapon arm bends toward that direction, the scattergun adds a supporting arm, and the emitter hand animates an orb. This creates responsive fictional character motion without modifying the supplied source images.
- Roguelike weapon-detail update: the aim rig uses shoulder-to-elbow-to-wrist anchors and hand markers rather than a floating rectangle. Each initial weapon has a distinct pixel-level silhouette: pistol (slide, grip, sight, muzzle cell), scattergun (stock, chamber, twin barrels, two-hand support), and emitter (wrist hub, four brackets, suspended core). Weapon position follows the natural grip and aim angle.
- Roguelike readability pass: desktop arena mode expands beyond the phone-width shell into a 16:9 combat window with compact top vitals/objective/currency HUD, bottom weapon-and-dash status, and no touch controls. Touch controls remain mobile-only. The first wave waits 10 seconds, telegraphs slow single-side low-damage enemies, and asks for 12 clears before the normal escalation. The three map templates are fixed tactical routes rather than random obstacle piles: Fog Harbor Platform (central loop), Power Corridor (breakable electrical boxes), and Faceless Platform (portal pressure). The minimap shows only player, cache, exit, elite, and Boss; off-screen threats use edge arrows and spawn circles. Upgrade selection pauses combat, then shows a one-second effect confirmation.
- Photo-based visual mapping is opt-in and applies only to consented adult photos. It uses user-confirmed non-sensitive visual cues (contour, gaze direction, lighting and desired likeness retention) to annotate the fictional observation record. It must not infer personality, identity, race, health, emotion, attractiveness, or MBTI from a face; uploads remain local to the current browser session in the prototype.
- Current Fog Harbor MVP scope: one wide 16:9 “雾港站台” arena, 11 timed clearance waves plus a 12th Boss wave, three normal hostile silhouettes (melee crawler, ranged heavy, self-destruct burst), elite variants, Boss, 20+ upgrade choices, and at least ten relics. The central route stays open; trains, pillars, breakable boxes, cables and barricades must form visible loopable routes rather than random dead ends.
- Combat UI must use only life, experience, wave objective and currency at the top, the battle field in the middle, and weapon/dash state at the bottom. Hide MBTI-style type text in combat; personality exists only in voluntary questionnaire, narrative dossiers and outside-combat records.
- Aim contract: resolve a locked enemy first (pointer-near target, otherwise nearest valid enemy), then use that same target for weapon angle, articulated hand rig, muzzle/core origin and projectile velocity. Draw a subtle lock reticle; target-outside-view arrows remain visible. Pistol fires from the right-hand muzzle, scattergun from its forward barrels, and emitter attacks from the forward wrist core / orbit nodes.
- Fog Harbor combat readability uses cyan-white for the protagonist and friendly fire, orange-red for enemy danger, gold for pickups, and red outlines plus a diamond marker for elite/Boss threats. Crawler, heavy and burst enemies use original transparent pixel-art worker silhouettes rather than circular tokens; station obstacles retain the same dark high-contrast pixel treatment.
- Offline release compatibility: the standalone entry is deliberately converted from Vite's module tag to a classic deferred script so double-click `file://` playback works. Any runtime art needed by that entry must be inlined; `scripts/prepare-offline.mjs` rejects a bundle containing `import.meta` instead of publishing a black-screen package.
- Fog Harbor art-slice direction: use high-detail original dark pixel art plus dynamic lighting, not a visible testing grid or flat collision rectangles. The opening camera frames a complete 16:9 painted station slice (rusted carriage, wet tile, rail, puddles, tickets, gates and a sparking electrical box); broader map tiles may remain simpler until they are upgraded. Render in four layers: ground texture, fixed scene props, units/projectiles, then fog/light/rain particles. Keep cyan-white for player/friendly fire, orange-red for threats, gold for pickups and red elite/Boss treatment. The three active hostile silhouettes are ticket-and-umbrella crawler, leaking faceless passenger and cable-wrapped conductor.
- Fog Harbor premium-arena pass: the playable sample is an authored 16:9 station rather than a freeform test field. Spawn sources are explicit train doors, side service corridors and lower rail fog, with a safe-distance fallback instead of player-adjacent spawning; the opening training wave uses only the west train door. Electric boxes are breakable collision props that open a route and emit a short cyan stun burst; puddles slow only the player moving across them. The old permanent grid is removed, and a desktop-only `碰撞` header toggle surfaces collision boxes, puddle bounds and spawn zones for development checks. The supplied protagonist art is rendered at readable 16:9 scale; weapon grips/muzzles are anchored at upper-torso/hand height and use a downward-aim lift so the gun cannot read as attached to the pelvis. Projectile origin reuses the exact same weapon pose.
- Projectile hit contract: use a raised, art-sized enemy projectile hitbox distinct from the smaller movement/contact collider, and test the full previous-to-current bullet segment rather than only the terminal bullet point. A projectile records hit enemy IDs so piercing rounds process each body once and in travel order. Every accepted hit produces a short cyan (or critical gold-white) impact spark, making hit confirmation visible.
- Full-map Fog Harbor rule: the current MVP is a compact one-screen 16:9 room, not an oversized black world with a decorative art slice. `WORLD_*` equals `VIEW_*`; the high-detail station plate fills the complete world at the bottom layer and the clamped camera never exposes space beyond it. Background-painted train, rails, walls, columns, gates and luggage use invisible `artBound` collision records; only runtime cabinets, markers, units and effects render above them. The central maintenance-light spawn is a safe visual anchor. The station gains escalating red emergency light from wave four onward; fog/rain, cabinet destruction and hit effects remain dynamic.
- Multi-station campaign update: each playable station is a complete 1280×720 original painted plate viewed through the 640×360 follow camera, creating four explorable camera sectors while never exposing a black world beyond the art. The live route is Fog Harbor Platform (waves 1–4) → 07 Power Corridor (waves 5–8) → Faceless Platform (waves 9–12 and Boss). Every station has scene-matched `artBound` collision for painted train/walls/rails/machinery, a small number of breakable electrical obstacles, authored puddles and spawn zones, a cache marker, and a full minimap outline. Clearing waves 4 or 8 grants a relic first, then unlocks an explicit in-world train-transfer panel; boarding preserves the current build, heals 14 HP, refills dashes and begins the next station. The new generated station plates must remain environment-only: no people, monsters, readable text, UI or fixed loot are painted into them.
- Enemy route reliability update: enemy spawn coordinates retain a minimum 42px inset from world edges before collision resolution. Every hostile uses a lightweight obstacle-aware steering fan (direct, shallow side steps, then wider escapes) rather than a single line toward the player. When forward motion is blocked, the NPC selects its strongest lateral escape; if it fails to make progress briefly, it flips its preferred side. This is deliberately simpler than a navigation mesh but keeps edge/corridor spawns flowing around authored station props at crowd scale.
- Resident worksite campaign update: `residentCampaigns.js` defines a three-stage work-map route for each of the 16 voluntary MBTI-style fictional residents. A stage has an original map title, environmental mechanic, temporary scene-art binding and a named next-station contact with role and line. Combat still reuses the three finished environmental plates until dedicated artwork is produced, but map identity, briefing cards, transition dialogue and future asset replacement are now data-driven per resident. Contacts are fictional dossier residents only; received user-supplied models may replace the current silhouette only after they are attached to the matching fictional contact.
- Train handoff update: clearing a transfer point and pressing board now enters a 2.6-second canvas interlude rather than switching maps immediately. It shows the protagonist boarding an illustrated carriage, a named contact silhouette holding the next-station light, its role/line and then automatically transfers the retained build to the next worksite. The combat header includes a user-controlled `快进 ×6` testing mode. It runs six fixed simulation steps per render interval, pauses naturally for upgrades/relics/transit prompts, and accelerates the boarding cutscene as well.
